The Timeline

1926

Julius Edgar Lilienfeld

The Austro-Hungarian physicist proposed the concept of the transistor. The transistor will become the basis of the modern microprocessor.

1936

Alan Turing

He produced the Turing Machine. It will lead to the construction of a computer to decipher the Enigma code.

1939

Bill Hewlett
David Packard

They created Hewlett-Packard also known as HP. HP was the first company created in the Silicon Valley. It was also the first company started in a garage.

1951

William Shockley
John Bardeen
Walter Houser Brattain

The work on the junction transistor was key in the developement of the microprocessor.

1957

The Traitorous Height

Julius Blank, Victor Grinich, Jean Hoerni, Eugene Kleiner, Jay Last, Gordon Moore, Robert Noyce and Sheldon Robert started Fairchild semiconductor. Gordon Moore and Robert Noyce would be a few years later the founder of Intel.

1968

Gordon Moore
Robert Noyce

It was the start of Intel computer. Intel was the first to release a widely available microprocessor. He was also the choice of IBM for the famous IBM PC.

1971

Marcian Hoff
Federico Faggin

The Intel 4004 was released and was the first commercially available microprocessor released by Intel.

1975

Chuck Peddle

The MOS 6502, who was a stripped down version of the Motorola 6800 was the enabler of the home computer revolution. All the famous 8 bits computers produced at that time were using a 6502. Atari 8bits , Commodore 8bits such as the C64, Apple II, etc were all using the 6502. It was the first very afordable microprocessor. It is still produced in 2022.

1978

Stephen P. Morse
Bruce Ravenel
Jim McKevitt
John Bayliss
Bill Pohlman

They were the creators and designers of the 8086 microprocessor. x86 will be the leading architecture used in the PC industry. It's one of the first CISC microprocessor available on the market.
