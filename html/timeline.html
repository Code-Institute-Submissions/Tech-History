The semiconductor timeline

1926

Julius Edgar Lilienfeld

The Austro-Hungarian physicist proposed the concept of Transistor. The transistor will became the basis of the modern microprocessor.

1936

Alan Turing

He produce the Turing Machine. It will led to the construction of a computer to decipher the Enigma code.

1939

Bill Hewlett & David Packard

They both created Hewlett-Packard also known as HP. HP was the first company created in the Silicon Valley. It was also the first one who started in a garage.

1951

William Shockley,

The work on the junction transistor was key in the developement of microprocessor.

1957

The Traitorous Height

Julius Blank, Victor Grinich, Jean Hoerni, Eugene Kleiner, Jay Last, Gordon Moore, Robert Noyce and Sheldon Robert start Fairchild semiconductor. Gordon Moore and Robert Noyce will then a few years later be the founder of Intel.

1968

Gordon Moore and Robert Noyce

1971

Marcian Hoff & Federico Faggin

the Intel 4004 was release and was the first commercialy available microprocessor released by Intel.

1975

Chuck Peddle

The MOS 6502 who was a strip down version of the Motorola 6800 led to the home computer revolution. All the famous 8 bits computers produced at that time were using a 6502. Atari 8bits, Commodore 8bits, Apple II, etc were all using the 6502.

1978

Stephen P. Morse, Bruce Ravenel, Jim McKevitt, John Bayliss and Bill Pohlman

They were the creator and designer of the x86 microprocessor. Leading the way to the PC era and the booming of the personal computer. It's one of the first CISC microprocessor available on the market.
